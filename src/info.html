Gran Canaria

Gran Canaria is an island located in the Atlantic Ocean, belonging to the Autonomous Community of the Canary Islands in Spain. Gran Canaria with an area of ​​1560.1 km² and an altitude of 1956 meters with Morro de la Agujereada, is the third island in the archipelago in terms of extension and altitude.

The island is one of the most important tourist destinations in Spain, with some 3.3 million tourists in 2011. Since 2005, a large sector of the island and its adjacent seafront, where its land part covers 46% (69 000 hectares) of the insular territory in its western half was declared a Biosphere Reserve by UNESCO. In 2019 it is declared the first and only UNESCO World Heritage Site of Gran Canaria and the province of Las Palmas, called "Risco Cultural Landscape Fallen and the Sacred Mountain Spaces of Gran Canaria ", covering an area of ​​18,000 hectares in the municipalities of Artenara, Tejeda, Gáldar and Agaete.

This island is called a "miniature continent" due to the different climates and variety of landscapes found, with long beaches and dunes of white sand, contrasting with green ravines and picturesque villages. A third of the island is under protection as a Biosphere Reserve by UNESCO. The number of annual visitors was 3.6 million in 2014 (of which 450.000 Spaniards). Most of the tourists visit the southern part of the island. The north tends to be cooler, while the south is warmer and sunny. The east coast of the island is flat, dotted with beaches, while the western coast is rockier and mountainous. The island possesses 32 Natural Protected Spaces, notably the Rural Park of Nublo, The Doramas Jungle, the Azuaje Ravine, Tamadaba, Pino Santo, etc.
